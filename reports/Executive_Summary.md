# 🎮 Level Balancing & Retention: Stakeholder Summary

**Status:** Action Required for Early-Game Retention

**Objective:** Optimize the "First Time User Experience" (FTUE) to reduce player churn between Levels 1–30.

### The Problem
Our data shows two primary "danger zones" in the current build that are likely causing players to quit before they reach our monetization or long-term engagement hooks:

1. **The "Boredom Trap" (Levels 11, 12, 15):** These levels are currently too easy. Players are over-performing by nearly 30%, meaning there is almost no risk of failure. This lack of challenge can lead high-value players to perceive the game as "for kids" or unengaging, causing them to drop off early.

2. **The "Level 20 Wall":** We are seeing a massive spike in difficulty specifically for Banana Collection goals after Level 20. Most players are failing to reach even 50% of the target. This creates a "hard stop" that frustrates players right as they are finishing their first session.

3. **Special Collectibles Friction:** These items are currently too rare or too hard to reach, making these specific levels feel "unfair" compared to standard distance-based levels.

### Recommended Roadmap
To smooth out the "Flow State" (keeping players challenged but not frustrated), I recommend the following immediate balance pass:

- **Tighten the Early Game:** Increase goal requirements for Levels 11, 12, and 15 by 20% to introduce a sense of mastery.

- **Lower the Wall:** Increase the move limit (swings/time) for Levels 20–30 by 15% to allow for more player errors without a "Game Over."

- **Boost "Feel Good" Moments:** Implement "pity spawns" or increase the density of Special Collectibles. This will increase level completion rates and positive reinforcement.

### Business Impact
By implementing these changes, we aim to stabilize our Day 1 and Day 7 Retention numbers. Smoothing the transition past Level 20 ensures a larger pool of players reaches the mid-game, where LTV (Lifetime Value) typically begins to scale.
